Personal Cards



Chase Sapphire Preferred

Signup bonus: 60,000 pts after spending $4000 in 3 months

Main Benefits:

  • 2x on travel and dining worldwide, 1x on everything else
  • DoorDash DashPass Membership (unlimited free deliveries and reduced service fees)
  • 10x on Lyft rides (for a limited time)
  • Points worth 25% more when redeeming using Chase Travel Portal
  • Transfer to partners such as United Airlines, Hyatt, Singapore Airlines etc
  • No Foreign Transaction Fee
  • Primary Auto Rental Collision Insurance
  • Extended Warranty and Purchase Protection
  • Trip Cancellation, Delay, and Lost Baggage Protection
  • Authorized users are free!
  • and more...

Why it works for me:

I currently do not have this card as both Sapphire products had the same bonus when I signed up. However, if you have a significant other that live in the same household, getting a Preferred is a great way for them to start earning points (bonus is worth $900+ if redeemed after upgrading to Reserve in year 2), then one of you can upgrade to the Reserve and pool your points together for higher redemption value.

Who is this for and when to apply?

The best all-one-one entry level travel credit card with fantastic perks and a modest annual fee. This allows you to grab the highest signup bonus available and make a decision later on to either upgrade to the higher-end Reserve for more benefits and redemption value, or to downgrade to a no annual fee card to retain your points without paying any fees.

Annual Fee: $95



Chase Sapphire Reserve

Signup bonus: 50,000 pts after spending $4000 in 3 months

Main Benefits:

  • 3x on travel and dining worldwide, 1x on everything else
  • $300 annual travel credit
  • Priority Pass Lounge Membership (1300+ lounges worldwide)
  • DoorDash DashPass Membership (unlimited free deliveries and reduced service fees)
  • Lyft Pink Membership (15% off rides, priority pickup, 10x on rides, etc)
  • Points worth 50% more when redeeming using Chase Travel Portal
  • Transfer to partners such as United Airlines, Hyatt, Singapore Airlines etc
  • No Foreign Transaction Fee
  • Primary Auto Rental Collision Insurance
  • Extended Warranty and Purchase Protection
  • Trip Cancellation, Delay, and Lost Baggage Protection
  • Global Entry / TSA Pre credit
  • Rental Car Privileges with National, Avis, and Silvercar
  • Luxury Hotel & Resort Collection (get free upgrades, dining credit, etc)
  • and more...

Why it works for me:

To start, the signup bonus alone is worth $750 when redeeming through the travel portal, and potentially more if transferred to a transfer partner for business class flights or high-end hotels. Furthermore, the $300 travel credit allows you to essentially get your money back for anything travel related (hotels, flights, parking, rideshare, transit, etc), bringing the annual fee down to $250. As someone who does not own a car, I ride Lyft often and the 10x points as well as 15% off, in addition to other perks such as 2 free bike or scooter rides every month, saves me at least $100 every year. Similarly, DoorDash’s membership allows me to save ~$10 per order, plus the $60 DoorDash credit, which essentially makes this card “profitable” for me. This does not factor in other extremely valuable benefits such as being able to access more than 1300 lounges worldwide with my family and friends, saving money on food purchases at the airport, getting expedited security and immigration screening at airports, free insurance on rental cars, discount when renting SilverCar, and the warranty and protections that reimburse me for expenses unforeseen circumstances such as delayed flight, lost baggage, damaged laptop happen.

Who is this for and when to apply?

I believe this is the ultimate all-in-one travel card for those who do not want to spend a lot of effort figuring out how to utilize complicated credits and rules (Amex). If you live in an urban area, and travel a couple times a year, this may be well worth it. Currently, the Preferred has a higher signup bonus with the same spend requirement. Chase currently allows you to only get the bonus on one of these cards, but not both, therefore the ideal strategy is to get the Preferred and potentially upgrade to the Reserve in year 2. This is because the Reserve allows the points to be redeemed at 50% more value when using their built-in Expedia portal, compared to 25% bonus on the Preferred. However, for those in a rush to get benefits such as lounge access, Global Entry credit, or in a rush to spend the bonus points before the first year, the Reserve may be a better option. I recommend reading through all the benefits on the official Chase website.

Annual Fee: $550
